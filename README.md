Cognitive Bandwidth Index — India

A composite index measuring the cognitive burden of poverty 
across 28 Indian states, built using NFHS-6 (2023-24) data.

What this measures
Five indicators combined into one score per state:
- Child stunting rate (higher = worse)
- Women with BMI below normal (higher = worse)
- Institutional birth rate (lower = worse)
- Women with bank accounts (lower = worse)
- Health insurance coverage (lower = worse)

Methodology
Min-max normalisation applied to each variable.
Higher-is-better variables reversed before normalisation.
Equal weighting across all five indicators.
CBI score = average of five normalised variables.

 Key findings
- Bihar (0.712) carries the highest cognitive burden
- Gujarat (0.674) ranks 3rd despite being considered economically advanced
- West Bengal (0.168) and Andhra Pradesh (0.173) perform best
- National average: 0.384

Data source
National Family Health Survey — NFHS-6 (2023-24)
Ministry of Health and Family Welfare, Government of India
